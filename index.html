What is Gunpla?

Gunpla, short for "Gundam Plastic Models," are highly detailed model kits produced by Bandai. These kits allow hobbyists to construct scale models of Mobile Suits from the Gundam anime franchise. Unlike traditional model kits, most Gunpla are snap-fit and do not require glue.

History of Gunpla

The first Gunpla kits were introduced in 1980, following the success of the original Mobile Suit Gundam series. Initially requiring glue, early kits evolved into snap-fit models in the 1990s, making the hobby more accessible. Over the years, Bandai has continued to innovate, releasing new grades, enhanced articulation, and improved details.

Gunpla Scales

Gunpla kits come in various scales, with the most common being:

  • 1/144 Scale: Typically 13 cm tall, compact and widely available.
  • 1/100 Scale: Around 16-19 cm tall, offering more details and articulation.
  • 1/60 Scale: Approximately 30 cm tall, providing impressive size and intricacy.
  • 1/48 Scale (Mega Size): Large kits with simplified assembly, ideal for display.
  • 1/200 Scale: Less common, usually found in older or collectible kits.
  • SD (Super Deformed): Small, stylized kits with exaggerated proportions.

Gunpla Grades

Gunpla kits are categorized into different grades, determining their complexity and level of detail:

  • HG (High Grade): A beginner-friendly grade with simplified parts and decent articulation.
  • RG (Real Grade): Advanced kits with intricate inner frames and greater articulation.
  • MG (Master Grade): Highly detailed 1/100 scale models featuring complex inner frames.
  • PG (Perfect Grade): The most intricate 1/60 scale models, offering premium details and articulation.
  • SD (Super Deformed): Small, exaggerated versions of Mobile Suits, ideal for casual builders.
  • Mega Size: Large-scale kits with simpler construction for display purposes.

The Process of Building Gunpla

Building a Gunpla model kit involves multiple steps to achieve the best result:

1. Nub Removal

Parts are cut from plastic runners using nippers, and remaining plastic nubs are smoothed out using a hobby knife or sanding tools.

2. Panel Lining

Fine-tip markers or washes are used to add depth to panel lines, enhancing the model's realism.

3. Decals and Stickers

Most kits include either stickers, dry transfers, or water-slide decals to enhance the appearance of the finished model.

4. Painting

Paint can be used to modify colors, add shading, or create battle damage for a more dynamic look.

5. Topcoat

A final topcoat (matte, semi-gloss, or gloss) is applied to protect the paint and decals while giving the model a consistent finish.

Why I Love Gunpla

  • Customization: The ability to modify and paint models to make them unique.
  • Detail and Articulation: Kits have impressive articulation, making them perfect for posing.
  • Focus and Relaxation: Building Gunpla helps me stay focused and relieves stress.
  • Displaying My Work: Once completed, models serve as an impressive display collection.
